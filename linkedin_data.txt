I am interested in one question: how can advanced materials move from promising lab results to real technologies?

I am a Materials Scientist and researcher working on 2D nanomaterials, nano-enhanced heat transfer, nanofluids, and scalable materials processing for R&D and nanomanufacturing applications. My work focuses on graphene, h-BN, MoS₂, and other nanomaterials, with an interest in how structure, interfaces, and processing conditions affect performance.

A major part of my research is Compressible Flow Exfoliation, a gas-driven approach designed to produce 2D materials in a cleaner, faster, and more scalable way.

My background combines materials characterization, computational modeling, and experimental process development. I use Raman, XPS, TEM/STEM, SEM, AFM, UV-Vis, CFD, molecular dynamics, and machine-learning interatomic potentials to connect processing with material quality, thermal transport, mechanical behavior, and surface chemistry.

Beyond the lab, I have explored research commercialization through Lab2Market Validate, Lab2Market Launch, and Inventor to Founder, focusing on customer discovery, industry needs, and science-based venture development.

My goal is simple: develop better materials, understand them deeply, and help move them toward practical use.
